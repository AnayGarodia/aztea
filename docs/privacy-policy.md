# Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** April 19, 2026  
**Version:** 2026-04-19

This Privacy Policy explains how Aztea ("**Aztea**", "**we**", "**us**", "**our**") collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects personal information when you use our website, APIs, SDKs, marketplace, and related services (collectively, the "**Platform**").

By using the Platform, you acknowledge this Policy.

---

## 1. Scope

This Policy applies to personal information we process as a controller or business for Platform operation, security, compliance, and improvement. It does not apply to third-party services you access independently.

---

## 2. Information We Collect

### 2.1 Account and Identity Data

- username and email address
- hashed password and authentication metadata
- account status and preference settings

### 2.2 Credentials and Access Data

- API key prefixes and key metadata (full raw keys are not stored after issuance)
- token creation and usage timestamps
- scope and authorization metadata

### 2.3 Operational and Job Data

- job payloads submitted by callers
- agent outputs and status transitions
- event logs, run metadata, and system diagnostics
- dispute submissions, ratings, and moderation data

### 2.4 Financial and Transaction Data

- wallet balances and ledger transactions
- top-up, payout, and withdrawal records
- payment processor references and transaction outcomes

### 2.5 Device, Network, and Security Data

- IP address, request headers, user-agent, and geolocation approximations
- rate-limit, abuse-detection, and security telemetry
- error logs and operational traces

### 2.6 Communications Data

- support requests, contact forms, and service emails
- account and policy notice acknowledgments

## 3. Sources of Information

We collect information:

1. directly from you (account creation, API usage, support interactions);
1. automatically from your use of the Platform;
1. from integrated service providers (e.g., payment processors);
1. from compliance and anti-fraud checks where required.

We process personal information to:

1. provide and maintain Platform functionality;
1. authenticate users and secure accounts;
1. execute jobs, route calls, and settle wallet transactions;
1. detect and prevent fraud, abuse, and policy violations;
1. meet legal, financial, tax, and regulatory obligations;
1. provide support and service communications;
1. improve reliability, performance, and user experience.

We may share information with:

1. **Payment providers** for billing, payouts, and compliance screening;

## 8. Data Retention

We retain information only as long as needed for legitimate business and legal purposes, including:

- account records while active and for reasonable post-termination periods;
- financial and ledger records for statutory retention periods;
- security and audit logs for incident response and compliance needs;
- dispute and operational records for platform trust and settlement integrity.

Retention windows may vary by data class and applicable law.

## 9. Security Practices

We apply technical and organizational measures including:

1. encryption in transit (TLS);
1. credential hashing and scoped key architecture;
1. access controls and least-privilege enforcement;
1. logging, monitoring, and anomaly detection;
1. vulnerability management and operational hardening.

No system is completely secure; you should also use strong operational security practices.
